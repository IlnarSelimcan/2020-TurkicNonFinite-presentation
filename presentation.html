Overview

Our argument in a nutshell:

  • Commonly assumed multifunctionality of Turkic non-finite verb forms does not hold
    • Conflates important distinctions
    • Too few categories
  • Proposal for novel delineation

The structure of the presentation:

  • Conventional delineation of Turkic non-finite systems
    • "participles"
    • "converbs"
  • Our proposed novel delineation
    • Overview
    • Methodology
    • Results
  • Outstanding issues

Conventional delineation

  • Participles - behave nominally and attributively
  • Converbs - pattern together with other verbs

Proposed novel delineation of Turkic NFV forms

  • Categorisation based on morphological and syntactic criteria
  • Assumed:
  • Four main categories:
    • Verbal noun
    • Verbal adjective
    • Infinitive
    • Verbal adverb

Turkic verbal nouns

  • Commonly conflated with verbal adjectives (both "participles")
  • Some nominal forms commonly called "infinitives"
  • Behave nominally
  • Potentially confused with nominalised verbal adjectives
    • МағанmɑʁɑnI.dat кітапханағаkɘtɑpχɑnɑʁɑlibrary-dat барғанbɑɾʁɑngo-vn ұнайды.kazʊnɑjdəbe.liked-npst-3 "I like going to the library."
    МағанmɑʁɑnI.dat кітапханағаkɘtɑpχɑnɑʁɑlibrary-dat барғанbɑɾʁɑngo-vadj кісіkɘsɘperson ұнайды.kazʊnɑjdəbe.liked-npst-3 "I like the/a person who went to the library."

Potential ambiguity: nominalisation of VADJ

    Кітапханағаkɘtɑpχɑnɑʁɑlibrary-dat барғандардыbɑrʁɑndɑrdəgo-vadj-pl-acc ұнатамын.kazʊnɑtɑmənbe.liked-caus-npst-1sg "I like goings to the library." (verbal noun reading) "I like [the ones] who go to the library." (verbal adjective reading)

Verbal adverbs vs. Infinitives

    Досумdosumfriend-poss.1sg китептиkiteptibook-acc китепканагаkitepqɑnɑʁɑlibrary-dat тапшырганыtɑpʃɯrʁɑnɯturn.in-inf турат.kirturɑtstand-npst-3 "My friend is getting ready to return the book to the library."
    Досумdosumfriend-poss.1sg китептиkiteptibook-acc китепканагаkitepqɑnɑʁɑlibrary-dat тапшырганыtɑpʃɯrʁɑnɯturn.in-vadv кезектеkezekteline-loc турат.kirturɑtstand-npst-3 "My friend is standing in line in order to return the book to the library."

Potential ambiguity: identical AUX and lexical verb

    Аһаанɑhɑːneat-inf барда.sahbɑrdɑgo-pst.3sg "S/he started to eat." Аһаанɑhɑːneat-vadv барда.sahbɑrdɑgo-pst.3sg "S/he ate and left."

Methodology

  • Chose 6 geographically diverse Turkic languages:
    Kazakh, Kyrgyz, Sakha, Tatar, Turkish, Tuvan
  • Consultation with grammars, native speakers, corpora
  • For each NFV-forming suffix, examined
    • morphological behaviour
    • syntactic behaviour
  • Output: near-exhaustive typology of NFV-forming suffixes

Historical perspective

  • vn + case → vadv
    • Verbal nouns with certain cases are adverbial
    • Can/do grammaticalise to verbal adverbs
      • when one of the components otherwise becomes less productive
      • when semantics of the combination are no longer compositional
    • Sometimes hard to tell when fully grammaticalised
      • -/GAnʧA/kir vadv (until _-ing) as -/GAn-ʧA/ vn-manner
  • vn ↔ vadj
    • Nouns and adjectives often used as overt heads of other's phrase in Turkic (though null head)
